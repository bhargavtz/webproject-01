WHAT IS PTE?

Heard of PTE? What is it? And why has it gained so much momentum in recent days?

The PTE is a much recently devised English language test similar to IELTS and TOEFL. It has been launched worldwide only in 2009. PTE is the Pearson’s Test of English which is being conducted by the Pearson Language Tests is the youngest one of its type which is accepted by most B-schools across the world.

The main aspects that keep it ahead in the race are that the results of this test are provided within five working days, which is really faster compared to both the IELTS and TOEFL. Also, the test results are more elaborately designed than those given for the IELTS. The thermometer-style Global Scale of English is employed to give an exact overview of the English language skills in various areas, such as grammar, fluency, pronunciation, and writing skills as well.

This test has gained acceptance by more than 6000 universities across the globe.

This computer-based test evaluates the proficiency of the candidate in the English Language and the overall scores range from is 10-90 points.

PTE Test Structure

The Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) has similar test sections of Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing in 2 versions of Academic and General training like in the IELTS. However, there are several distinctive features in the PTE Academic exam format which make it stand apart from IELTS.

In IELTS, the examiner evaluates the speaking abilities of the candidates by making live online conversation whereas in PTE, there is no live conversation and the candidate has to speak to a computerized system for recording which is then evaluated by a computer program to rate the test taker. However, PTE is entirely computer-based and therefore uses many creative techniques to test the writing capabilities of the candidate, unlike the IELTS that uses a paper-based format for the conduct of the test.

IELTS has a specific time for each paper, and test-takers can move across sections and can attempt any question, whereas PTE has set a stipulated time for each module and has to only follow the chronology to complete the test
